State government benifits

Karnataka Self Employment scheme

Under this scheme, Subsidies will be provided to the religious minority communities with the help of Nationalized / Scheduled banks to start or improve a small-scale handicraft industry, service sector, and agro-based activities.

Last updated 3 mins ago
Ganga Kalyan scheme

Each beneficiary should have 1 acre 20 gunte (1 acre 50 cents) of acre to 5 acres of dry land, in districts like Kodagu, Dakshina Kannada, Udupi, Uttara Kannada, Chikkamagaluru, Shimoga and Hassan, the minimum land should be 1 acre as the availability of land is very less.

Last updated 3 mins ago
Thayi Bhagya

Under this scheme, the pregnant woman belonging to BPL family can avail delivery services free of cost in the registered private hospital near her house. She is not required to pay any charges right from the point of admission to discharge.

Last updated 3 mins ago
Ksheera Bhagya scheme

Students in classes 1 to 10 in government and aided schools get milk under the Ksheera Bhagya scheme. Milk is served five days a week. The department needs 250 metric tonnes of milk powder every year to supply milk under the Ksheera Bhagya scheme.

Last updated 3 mins ago
Gram Vikash Scheme

Gram Vikash Scheme is a village development programme to transform a village into a selfdependent. village by organizing various village level meetings, awareness and training. programme among the village people.

Last updated 3 mins ago
Soura Belaku scheme

By this programme, the personnel appointed for maintenance and repair works by the panchayats and other rural youth will be given training in maintenance and repair works of solar street lights. Trainees will also be educated on Energy Conservation and other renewable energy sources.
